
#  AI-Powered Burnout Prediction

## 📌 The Problem: The "Silent Attrition" and Invisible Micro-Stressors

Companies lose their best talent not to a lack of skill, but to **Burnout**. Through our analysis of over **800,000 employee records**, we discovered that burnout is a "silent leak" it stems from daily, subtle accumulations of work pressure that managers often fail to quantify until it's too late.

The challenge is that traditional management only looks at output, missing the **complex interactions** between workload, role complexity, and the "protective shields" of team culture.

## 💡 The Solution: Data-Driven Precision with Engineered Insights
**The Informed Burnout Prediction** isn't just a predictor; it's a strategic radar. By moving beyond raw data into **Feature Engineering**, the system uncovers the mechanics of employee strain:

1. **Strategic Feature Selection:** We streamlined 31 variables down to **11 critical features**, eliminating noise and "Data Leakage" (e.g., removing `stress_level` to ensure the model predicts, rather than replicates, the outcome).
2. **Advanced Engineering (The "Culture Shield"):** * **Culture Shield Index:** Quantifies how team sentiment neutralizes work pressure (our study found a strong -0.57 correlation with burnout).
   * **Pressure Index:** Combines role complexity and workload to pinpoint the exact "tipping point" for exhaustion.
   * **Effort Efficiency:** Measures if overtime hours are actually translating into goal achievement.
3. **Interactive Management Hub:** A **Streamlit Dashboard** that visualizes these metrics, allowing managers to intervene before a "Culture Shield" breaks.

## 🚀 Key Performance Insights (From EDA Phase)
* **The Culture Shield Factor:** High team sentiment is the most powerful defense against burnout.
* **The Overtime Trap:** Employees with high overtime but low emotional returns (satisfaction) show the highest propensity for turnover.
* **Organizational Variation:** Burnout patterns vary significantly across the **38 unique divisions**, requiring department-specific intervention strategies.
